# Product Page Optimizer

## Purpose

Improve product pages so visitors quickly understand the product, believe the claims, compare options, overcome objections, and move toward purchase.

This skill is built for DTC websites, e-commerce stores, product catalogs, international product pages, and marketplace-linked brand sites.

## When to Use

Use this skill when the task is to:

- improve a product page;
- audit product page conversion;
- rewrite product page structure;
- clarify benefits and proof;
- improve product descriptions;
- reduce purchase objections;
- organize images, claims, FAQs, and CTAs.

## When NOT to Use

Do not use when the user only needs homepage optimization, checkout optimization, technical SEO, or marketplace-specific card optimization. Use the relevant specialist skill for those tasks.

## Inputs

Useful inputs:

- product name;
- product category;
- current product page copy or URL;
- target customer;
- product features;
- benefits;
- proof points;
- price;
- reviews or objections;
- images or media;
- desired CTA;
- traffic source.

## Workflow

1. Identify the product page goal and target buyer.
2. Check first-screen clarity: product, benefit, proof, price, CTA.
3. Translate features into customer benefits.
4. Build a clear claim and proof hierarchy.
5. Identify objections and missing answers.
6. Recommend page section order.
7. Improve CTA, trust signals, reviews, FAQs, and comparison logic.
8. Prioritize fixes by conversion impact.

## Output

Always provide:

- product page diagnosis;
- first-screen recommendation;
- benefit hierarchy;
- claim-proof map;
- objection fixes;
- recommended section order;
- CTA improvements;
- FAQ ideas;
- priority fixes;
- next steps.

## Best Practices

- Lead with the main customer outcome.
- Show proof near every important claim.
- Make price and value easy to understand.
- Use FAQs to remove objections.
- Make product images explain, not just decorate.
- Keep CTAs visible and consistent.

## Common Mistakes

- Listing ingredients or features without benefits.
- Hiding proof below the fold.
- Weak product title or hero block.
- Too many unsupported claims.
- Missing FAQs for common objections.
- Product page looks good but does not sell.
