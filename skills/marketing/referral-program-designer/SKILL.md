# Referral Program Designer

## Purpose

Design practical referral programs that turn satisfied customers, partners, creators, distributors, or community members into a repeatable acquisition channel.

This skill helps define the referral audience, incentive, mechanics, message, tracking, fraud controls, and launch plan.

## When to Use

Use this skill when the task is to:

- create a referral program;
- improve word-of-mouth growth;
- design customer referral incentives;
- create creator or affiliate-style referral logic;
- build B2B partner referral mechanics;
- increase repeat purchase and advocacy;
- turn loyal customers into promoters.

## When NOT to Use

Do not use when the user only needs influencer outreach, paid ads, or generic loyalty discounts. Use Retention Specialist first if customers are not yet satisfied or repeat purchase is weak.

## Inputs

Useful inputs:

- product or service;
- target referrer;
- target referred customer;
- average order value;
- margin or budget;
- current customer satisfaction signals;
- channel for sharing;
- available tracking tools;
- legal or marketplace constraints.

## Workflow

1. Define who should refer and who should be referred.
2. Identify the referral motivation and trust trigger.
3. Choose the incentive model.
4. Define the referral action and conversion path.
5. Create clear program rules.
6. Create referral messages and assets.
7. Define tracking, attribution, and fraud checks.
8. Plan launch, testing, and optimization.

## Output

Always provide:

- referral program concept;
- target referrer and referred customer;
- incentive structure;
- program rules;
- referral message;
- launch plan;
- tracking logic;
- fraud or abuse risks;
- KPI table;
- next steps.

## Best Practices

- Make the referral action very easy.
- Reward behavior that creates real value.
- Match the incentive to margin and customer motivation.
- Give referrers simple words to share.
- Track both referral volume and referred customer quality.
- Start with a small test before scaling.

## Common Mistakes

- Offering incentives that destroy margin.
- Making the program hard to understand.
- Rewarding low-quality or fake referrals.
- Launching before customer satisfaction is strong.
- Forgetting to remind customers at the right moment.
