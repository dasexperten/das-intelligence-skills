# Pricing Strategy Expert

## Purpose

Create practical pricing strategy recommendations that connect customer value, positioning, margin, competitors, channel rules, discounts, bundles, and growth goals.

This skill helps choose, explain, and test prices for e-commerce, marketplaces, B2B export, DTC websites, launches, and premium product positioning.

## When to Use

Use this skill when the task is to:

- set or review pricing;
- compare price tiers;
- plan discounts or promotions;
- design bundles;
- adapt prices by market or channel;
- protect margin;
- connect pricing to positioning and perceived value.

## When NOT to Use

Do not use as a full financial model, tax model, or legal pricing review. Use this skill to guide practical marketing and commercial pricing decisions.

## Inputs

Useful inputs:

- product or service;
- target customer;
- market or channel;
- current price;
- cost and margin;
- competitor prices;
- perceived value;
- positioning;
- discount rules;
- sales goal;
- constraints.

## Workflow

1. Define the pricing goal.
2. Identify the target customer and value perception.
3. Review costs, margin, and channel constraints.
4. Compare competitor and substitute pricing.
5. Choose pricing logic: premium, parity, penetration, bundle, anchor, or value-based.
6. Identify discount risks and guardrails.
7. Recommend tests or price ladders.
8. Define KPIs and decision rules.

## Output

Always provide:

- pricing diagnosis;
- recommended pricing logic;
- competitor context;
- margin and discount notes;
- bundle or tier ideas;
- test plan;
- KPI table;
- risks;
- next steps.

## Best Practices

- Price should match positioning and proof.
- Protect margin before scaling promotions.
- Use bundles to increase perceived value.
- Separate launch discounts from permanent pricing.
- Test price changes with clear decision rules.

## Common Mistakes

- Copying competitor prices without context.
- Discounting before fixing value communication.
- Ignoring marketplace commission and logistics.
- Making premium claims with weak proof.
- Running promotions without margin guardrails.
