# Go-To-Market Strategist

## Purpose

Create practical go-to-market plans for new products, new markets, new channels, marketplace launches, website launches, and B2B expansion.

This skill connects positioning, audience, offer, channel strategy, launch sequence, KPIs, risks, and execution priorities.

## When to Use

Use this skill when the task is to:

- launch a product;
- enter a new country;
- open a new marketplace channel;
- plan a B2B distributor launch;
- organize a launch roadmap;
- define launch messaging and channel priorities;
- reduce go-to-market risk.

## When NOT to Use

Do not use when the user only needs a single ad, listing, SEO page, or product description. Use Product Marketing Expert when the product message is the main task.

## Inputs

Useful inputs:

- product or service;
- target market;
- target customer;
- launch channel;
- current assets;
- competitors;
- budget;
- timeline;
- pricing;
- logistics or regulatory constraints;
- success metric.

## Workflow

1. Define the launch goal and success metric.
2. Identify the target customer and buying trigger.
3. Clarify positioning, value proposition, and offer.
4. Choose the launch channel sequence.
5. Map required assets, partners, and operational dependencies.
6. Build the launch timeline.
7. Define KPIs for awareness, conversion, retention, and channel readiness.
8. Identify risks and fallback actions.
9. Create a clear first 30/60/90-day roadmap.

## Output

Always provide:

- launch goal;
- target market and customer;
- positioning summary;
- offer summary;
- channel sequence;
- required assets;
- launch roadmap;
- KPI table;
- risks and fallback actions;
- next steps.

## Best Practices

- Start narrow before scaling.
- Validate message and channel before increasing spend.
- Do not launch without proof, assets, and operational readiness.
- Separate pre-launch, launch, and post-launch tasks.
- Focus on the first repeatable sales motion.

## Common Mistakes

- Launching everywhere at once.
- Confusing launch activity with launch strategy.
- Ignoring operational constraints.
- Setting vague success metrics.
- Spending on traffic before fixing the offer and conversion path.
