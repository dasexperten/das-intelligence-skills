# Community Marketing Expert

## Purpose

Build practical community marketing systems that create trust, repeat engagement, feedback, advocacy, referrals, creator participation, and long-term brand affinity.

This skill helps turn customers, fans, creators, partners, or professionals into an active audience around a clear shared interest.

## When to Use

Use this skill when the task is to:

- create a community marketing plan;
- build brand community;
- improve customer engagement;
- organize creator or ambassador participation;
- collect feedback and insights;
- increase trust and advocacy;
- support referrals and retention.

## When NOT to Use

Do not use when the user only needs one social media post, one influencer campaign, or a paid ads plan. Use Social Content skills or Creator Outreach skills for narrow execution.

## Inputs

Useful inputs:

- brand or product;
- target community members;
- shared interest or identity;
- platform;
- current audience;
- content themes;
- engagement signals;
- incentives;
- moderation constraints;
- business goal.

## Workflow

1. Define the community purpose and business goal.
2. Identify the target members and shared motivation.
3. Choose the community platform and participation format.
4. Define content pillars and recurring rituals.
5. Create engagement loops: ask, share, answer, feature, reward.
6. Design member roles such as customer, expert, creator, ambassador, or partner.
7. Add moderation rules and quality standards.
8. Define KPIs for engagement, trust, retention, referrals, and feedback.
9. Build a launch and maintenance plan.

## Output

Always provide:

- community concept;
- target members;
- shared motivation;
- platform recommendation;
- content pillars;
- engagement rituals;
- member roles;
- moderation rules;
- KPI table;
- launch plan;
- next steps.

## Best Practices

- Build around a shared problem, identity, or aspiration.
- Give members reasons to participate, not only consume.
- Feature members and user stories.
- Keep rituals simple and repeatable.
- Protect quality with clear moderation rules.
- Connect community activity to retention, referrals, research, and content.

## Common Mistakes

- Creating a group with no clear purpose.
- Posting only brand announcements.
- Treating community as free advertising.
- Ignoring moderation and trust.
- Measuring only member count instead of participation quality.
