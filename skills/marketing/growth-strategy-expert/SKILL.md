# Growth Strategy Expert

## Purpose

Create practical growth strategies that identify where growth can come from, which levers matter most, and what actions should be tested first.

This skill connects acquisition, conversion, retention, pricing, offers, product experience, marketplaces, website, content, and sales into a focused growth plan.

## When to Use

Use this skill when the task is to:

- find growth opportunities;
- prioritize marketing actions;
- improve revenue or repeat purchase;
- build a growth roadmap;
- diagnose why growth is slow;
- select experiments;
- connect channels into a growth system.

## When NOT to Use

Do not use when the user only needs one campaign, one ad, one product page, or a detailed financial forecast. Use specialist skills for narrow execution.

## Inputs

Useful inputs:

- business goal;
- current revenue or sales signals;
- channels;
- customer segments;
- conversion data;
- retention data;
- competitors;
- budget;
- timeline;
- constraints.

## Workflow

1. Define the growth goal and time horizon.
2. Identify the main growth constraint: traffic, conversion, retention, price, trust, awareness, or operations.
3. Map current growth channels and customer journey.
4. Find high-impact levers across acquisition, conversion, retention, and monetization.
5. Separate quick wins from strategic foundations.
6. Prioritize experiments by impact, effort, confidence, and learning value.
7. Build a 30/60/90-day growth roadmap.
8. Define metrics and decision rules.
9. Add risks and dependencies.

## Output

Always provide:

- growth diagnosis;
- main constraint;
- growth levers;
- prioritized experiments;
- 30/60/90-day roadmap;
- KPI table;
- risks and dependencies;
- next actions.

## Best Practices

- Solve the biggest constraint first.
- Do not add more traffic to a weak conversion path.
- Treat retention as a growth lever, not an afterthought.
- Use experiments to learn, not just to be busy.
- Keep the roadmap executable for the available team.

## Common Mistakes

- Trying every channel at once.
- Confusing activity with growth.
- Ignoring unit economics.
- Optimizing small details before fixing the main constraint.
- Running experiments without decision rules.
