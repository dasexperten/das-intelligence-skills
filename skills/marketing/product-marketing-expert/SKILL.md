# Product Marketing Expert

## Purpose

Turn product features, customer needs, positioning, proof, and channel context into clear product marketing assets that help launch, explain, sell, and scale a product.

This skill connects strategy with execution across websites, marketplaces, ads, content, sales decks, and distributor communication.

## When to Use

Use this skill when the task is to:

- create product messaging;
- prepare a product launch;
- explain product benefits;
- build product page content;
- create marketplace selling points;
- prepare B2B distributor materials;
- translate product features into customer value;
- compare product variants.

## When NOT to Use

Do not use when the user only needs brand strategy, technical SEO, paid ads setup, or legal claim validation. Use this skill after the product and target market are clear.

## Inputs

Useful inputs:

- product name;
- product category;
- target customer;
- market or channel;
- features;
- ingredients or technology;
- benefits;
- proof points;
- competitors;
- price tier;
- launch goal.

## Workflow

1. Define the product, market, and buyer.
2. Identify the main customer problem or desired outcome.
3. Translate features into benefits and proof.
4. Define the product's core message.
5. Build a hierarchy of primary, secondary, and support claims.
6. Adapt messaging for website, marketplace, ads, social, and B2B.
7. Identify objections and required proof.
8. Recommend launch or improvement actions.

## Output

Always provide:

- product diagnosis;
- target customer;
- core message;
- feature-to-benefit map;
- proof points;
- objection handling;
- channel messaging;
- launch or optimization actions.

## Best Practices

- Start with the customer problem, not the product feature list.
- Use one primary message and a small set of support claims.
- Make proof visible wherever the claim appears.
- Adapt the same idea for each channel instead of copying one text everywhere.
- Keep sales claims clear, useful, and supportable.

## Common Mistakes

- Listing features without customer outcomes.
- Making too many claims at once.
- Ignoring objections.
- Using technical language where simple benefit language is needed.
- Treating marketplace content and B2B content as the same task.
