# Brand Positioning Expert

## Purpose

Create or sharpen a clear market position for a brand, product, or product line so customers quickly understand why it matters, who it is for, and why it is better than alternatives.

## When to Use

Use this skill when the task is to:

- define brand positioning;
- reposition a product;
- enter a new market;
- differentiate from competitors;
- improve website, marketplace, or sales messaging;
- clarify the category and buying reason.

## When NOT to Use

Do not use when the user only needs a slogan, ad copy, SEO keywords, or a full brand strategy. Use Brand Strategy Expert for wider brand platform work.

## Inputs

Useful inputs:

- product or brand name;
- category;
- country or marketplace;
- target customer;
- competitors;
- current claims;
- strongest proof points;
- price tier.

## Workflow

1. Identify the real category where the customer compares alternatives.
2. Define the highest-value customer segment.
3. List the main alternatives and their default promises.
4. Find the brand's strongest believable advantage.
5. Convert the advantage into customer value.
6. Write one positioning statement.
7. Translate it into channel messages for website, marketplaces, ads, and B2B.
8. Flag weak claims, category clichés, and proof gaps.

## Output

Always provide:

- category frame;
- target customer;
- competitive alternatives;
- core differentiation;
- proof points;
- positioning statement;
- channel translation;
- next actions.

## Best Practices

- Position against a real alternative, not against everyone.
- Use proof before poetry.
- Choose a position that can be repeated across channels.
- Keep one main idea; secondary benefits support it.
- Make the position commercially useful, not just elegant.

## Common Mistakes

- Saying “premium quality” without proof.
- Targeting everyone.
- Confusing ingredient features with customer value.
- Copying category language from competitors.
- Creating a position that does not fit the price or channel.
