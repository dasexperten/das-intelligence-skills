# Market Entry Strategist

## Purpose

Create practical market-entry strategies for launching a brand, product, marketplace channel, distributor model, or local website in a new country or region.

This skill connects market attractiveness, customer needs, competitors, channels, pricing, regulation, logistics, localization, partners, risks, and first-step execution.

## When to Use

Use this skill when the task is to:

- enter a new country;
- compare market-entry options;
- plan distributor or marketplace expansion;
- localize positioning;
- identify launch risks;
- choose first channel and first offer;
- build a phased entry roadmap.

## When NOT to Use

Do not use as legal, tax, or customs advice. Use this skill for commercial strategy and planning, then verify legal and regulatory details with qualified local experts.

## Inputs

Useful inputs:

- country or region;
- product category;
- target customer;
- current brand assets;
- competitors;
- price tier;
- channels;
- regulatory constraints;
- logistics constraints;
- budget;
- timeline;
- success metric.

## Workflow

1. Define the market-entry goal.
2. Assess market attractiveness and constraints.
3. Identify customer segment and local buying triggers.
4. Analyze competitors and substitutes.
5. Choose entry model: distributor, marketplace, DTC, retail, B2B, or hybrid.
6. Adapt positioning, offer, pricing, and proof.
7. Map operational dependencies.
8. Build a phased entry roadmap.
9. Define KPIs, risks, and validation steps.

## Output

Always provide:

- market-entry goal;
- market diagnosis;
- target customer;
- competitor context;
- recommended entry model;
- localized positioning and offer;
- pricing and channel notes;
- operational dependencies;
- phased roadmap;
- risks and validation steps;
- next actions.

## Best Practices

- Start with one focused entry path.
- Validate demand before scaling logistics and inventory.
- Localize proof, not only language.
- Match channel choice to customer behavior.
- Use partners when local trust, compliance, or distribution is critical.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating every country the same.
- Launching before regulatory and logistics dependencies are clear.
- Copying home-market messaging without localization.
- Choosing a distributor without evaluation criteria.
- Scaling inventory before channel-market fit is tested.
