# Google Ads CPC Quality Score Optimizer

## Purpose

Lower unnecessary CPC and improve paid-search efficiency through stronger query relevance, expected CTR, ad relevance, landing-page experience, and auction strategy.

## When to Use

Use this skill when CPC is rising, Quality Score components are weak, CTR is low, ads are poorly matched to intent, or traffic cost is high relative to conversion value.

## Inputs

Useful inputs: keywords, search terms, average CPC, CTR, conversion rate, Quality Score, expected CTR status, ad relevance status, landing-page experience status, ads, and landing pages.

## Workflow

1. Segment CPC by keyword, query, match type, device, geography, and campaign.
2. Review expected CTR, ad relevance, and landing-page experience.
3. Tighten intent grouping and remove irrelevant traffic.
4. Improve ad-to-query and landing-page alignment.
5. Review bidding and auction pressure only after relevance issues are addressed.
6. Track CPC together with conversion rate, CPA, and value.

## Output

Always provide CPC diagnosis, Quality Score component review, expensive query list, relevance fixes, landing-page notes, and next actions.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating low CPC as the final goal.
- Pausing expensive high-value queries without profitability context.
- Combining unrelated intent in one ad group.
- Trying to fix landing-page problems only through bids.
